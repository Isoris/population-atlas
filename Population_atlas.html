Structure design — two rounds
scaffold · awaiting MODULE_2B
Hatchery cohorts violate Hardy-Weinberg by construction (siblings, full broodlines, related broodstock). Two-round structure analysis is required:
  1. Round 1 — all 226 samples → reflects family + broodline structure. Useful for visualizing the actual cohort composition; not a population-genetic K estimate.
  2. Round 2 — NAToRA-pruned 81 unrelated samples → cleaner K estimate for the population-level structure, free of within-family signal.
K clusters in this dataset reflect hatchery broodline structure, not species admixture. The cohort is pure C. gariepinus.

Hatchery health F_ROH × H plane · diversity-contextualised inbreeding
Stock-level verdict combining autozygosity (F_ROH) and species-baseline-referenced heterozygosity (H / Href) · the quadrant determines the verdict
⚠
New feature · no comparator baseline yet
F_ROH | H (diversity-contextualised F_ROH) is a novel framework introduced in this manuscript. It has no published comparator dataset against which a cohort's quadrant verdict can be externally validated. The four verdict labels (Healthy / Recent inbreeding only / Diversity erosion only / Caution) are interpretive aids derived from the F_ROH and H values alone, not field-validated thresholds. Quadrant cuts are computed from the median of loaded cohorts, so adding or removing comparators changes them. Use the verdicts as a structured way to think about your stock—not as a regulatory or conservation-grade classification.
Why both numbers, not just F_ROH?
framework
F_ROH alone is ambiguous across studies. A cohort with F_ROH = 0.30 on a diversity-replete background reflects recent close-kin inbreeding only and is manageable by outcrossing. The same F_ROH = 0.30 layered on top of already-eroded heterozygosity reflects sustained demographic decline — outcrossing within the stock cannot recover the missing diversity because the non-ROH genome itself is depleted.
Reporting F_ROH and H jointly resolves this ambiguity. Every stock becomes a point in 2D space; the position tells you which biological process is driving the inbreeding signal and which management response is appropriate. The four quadrants of the plane and their verdicts are shown below.

Methods note
manuscript-ready
We propose that interpretation of F_ROH in managed populations requires explicit reference to baseline heterozygosity, a framing we term diversity-contextualised F_ROH (F_ROH | H). Under this framing, an F_ROH value is reported together with the cohort's per-site H expressed as a fraction of a reference or wild-population estimate; populations with identical F_ROH but different H | H_ref ratios reflect distinct underlying processes rather than equivalent inbreeding states. The four quadrants of the plane (low/high F_ROH \u00d7 low/high H | H_ref) provide a stock-level health verdict that distinguishes recent pedigree events (high F_ROH at preserved H) from sustained demographic erosion (high F_ROH at depleted H). The hatchery-health verdict is exported to the Breeding page as a Diversity-source row so the breeding committee can see stock-level context alongside per-sample flags.

Glossary
documented
term
definition
broodline
Hatchery breeding line (parent stock identity); typically 3-5 broodlines per cohort. Each broodline contributes a distinct genetic background.
family
Single full-sibling group from one cross. A broodline contains multiple families.
F_ROH
Inbreeding coefficient estimated from runs of homozygosity. F_ROH = (sum of ROH lengths) / (callable genome length).
ROH
Run of homozygosity — a contiguous stretch of homozygous genotypes. Long ROH (>5 Mb) indicates recent inbreeding; short ROH (<1 Mb) indicates ancient.
θπ
Pairwise nucleotide diversity per site (Tajima's π). The pestPG tP column. The diversity proxy used throughout this atlas.
H_obs
Observed heterozygosity — fraction of genotypes that are heterozygous. Distinct from θπ but related; H_obs is a per-genotype count, θπ is a per-site expected pairwise difference.
NAToRA
Hungarian-algorithm kinship-pruning tool. Removes minimum samples needed so that no remaining pair has π̂ above a threshold (here 3rd-degree, π̂ ≥ 0.0884).
NGSadmix
Ancestry-decomposition tool that estimates per-sample Q (admixture proportions) at user-specified K from genotype likelihoods.
folded SFS
Site-frequency spectrum where minor and major alleles are not distinguished (no outgroup polarization). Required when no clean ancestral state is available.
callable genome
Genome regions where joint variant calling is statistically reliable (post-mask: low complexity, mappability, coverage thresholds).
