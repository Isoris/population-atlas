What this is
v1 · structure committed
The Population Atlas is a sibling deliverable to the Local PCA Scrubber (Inversions Atlas). Same dataset (226-sample C. gariepinus hatchery cohort), same design language, different focus.
The scrubber is an exploratory tool — you scrub windows, lock colors, promote candidates, test recluster modes against contingency. Active analysis. This atlas is a summary view — you don't explore, you read. Where the cohort comes from, what it looks like, what its diversity / inbreeding patterns are. The two together cover the population-genetic side of the thesis.
Four-atlas family — the manuscript design (ADR-14)
design intent
Four sibling HTML deliverables, one per audience. Each is one focused view of the full results. Together they cover the manuscript.

Why pages 4 / 5 / 6 are summary-only
scope decision
Pages 4 (Heterozygosity), 5 (Diversity), 6 (Inbreeding) used to be scaffolds promising deep deliverables. The Diversity Atlas now ships those deliverables in production:
  • All 19 MODULE_3 supplementary tables loaded as JSON-backed sortable interactive views
  • 22 SVG plots: distributions, scatters, per-chromosome bars, hotspot heatmaps, cluster boxes with strip-plot overlays, K-sweep trajectories, the genome-wide θπ landscape, the per-sample × hotspot heatmap, the ROH composition stacked plot, the chr × sample F_ROH heatmap, plus QC plots and pruning comparisons
  • Drill-down per sample with cohort percentiles + automatic flag notes
Two atlases, two depths, one source of truth (MODULE_3 supp tables). The Population Atlas keeps its three pages as cohort-QC landing cards for breeding-program collaborators who want the topline numbers without diving deep — clicking the green callout takes them straight to the Diversity Atlas's matching tab.

Glossary
documented
term
definition
broodline
Hatchery breeding line (parent stock identity); typically 3-5 broodlines per cohort. Each broodline contributes a distinct genetic background.
family
Single full-sibling group from one cross. A broodline contains multiple families.
F_ROH
Inbreeding coefficient estimated from runs of homozygosity. F_ROH = (sum of ROH lengths) / (callable genome length).
ROH
Run of homozygosity — a contiguous stretch of homozygous genotypes. Long ROH (>5 Mb) indicates recent inbreeding; short ROH (<1 Mb) indicates ancient.
θπ
Pairwise nucleotide diversity per site (Tajima's π). The pestPG tP column. The diversity proxy used throughout this atlas.
H_obs
Observed heterozygosity — fraction of genotypes that are heterozygous. Distinct from θπ but related; H_obs is a per-genotype count, θπ is a per-site expected pairwise difference.
NAToRA
Hungarian-algorithm kinship-pruning tool. Removes minimum samples needed so that no remaining pair has π̂ above a threshold (here 3rd-degree, π̂ ≥ 0.0884).
NGSadmix
Ancestry-decomposition tool that estimates per-sample Q (admixture proportions) at user-specified K from genotype likelihoods.
folded SFS
Site-frequency spectrum where minor and major alleles are not distinguished (no outgroup polarization). Required when no clean ancestral state is available.
callable genome
Genome regions where joint variant calling is statistically reliable (post-mask: low complexity, mappability, coverage thresholds).

What’s new in v1.3
v1.3 · turn 81
One addition since v1.2:
  • Page 7 — Hatchery health (new tab, between Inbreeding and Breeding). Operationalises the manuscript’s F_ROH × H plane framework (introduced in the Discussion as diversity-contextualised F_ROH, F_ROH | H). Each cohort sits in 2D (F_ROH, H/H_ref) space; the quadrant determines a colored verdict: Healthy / Recent inbreeding only / Diversity erosion only / Caution — historical erosion + recent inbreeding. Inline SVG scatter, comparator panel, JSON/TSV upload. The focal cohort’s verdict is surfaced as a Diversity-source row on the breeding page so the committee sees stock-level health alongside per-sample flags. Coloring rule: only the verdict text is colored — cohort names, F_ROH, and H values stay in normal ink. Breeding renumbered 7 → 8; About renumbered 8 → 9.
See the hatchery health page’s in-page documentation for the framework rationale, quadrant verdicts, and JSON schema.
What’s new in v1.2
v1.2 · turn 79

Auto-derives rows from cohort state when JSON layers load; accepts breeding_highlights.json/.tsv overlay.
